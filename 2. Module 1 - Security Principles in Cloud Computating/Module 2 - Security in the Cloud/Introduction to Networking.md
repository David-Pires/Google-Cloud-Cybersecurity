### üá∫üá∏ English Version
This section explores how devices connect and the different scales of network infrastructure.

* **Networking Equipment:** Devices like computers, servers, and printers are called **endpoints** or **hosts**. They connect to **switches** and **routers** to direct traffic. Switches are modern standards, while hubs are legacy technology.
* **SOHO Networks (Small Office/Home Office):** Small networks with few devices. Often, the router, switch, and Wi-Fi access point are integrated into a single physical device.
* **Enterprise & SMB Networks:**
    * **Enterprise:** Large-scale networks (e.g., banks) with hundreds of devices across multiple buildings or cities.
    * **SMB (Small to Medium Business):** Similar to enterprise but smaller in scale and focused on cost-effective equipment.
* **LAN (Local Area Network):** A network confined to a local area, such as a home, office, or a single building.
* **WAN (Wide Area Network):** Connects geographically distant networks (e.g., offices in different cities). Service providers are typically hired to provide WAN connectivity.
