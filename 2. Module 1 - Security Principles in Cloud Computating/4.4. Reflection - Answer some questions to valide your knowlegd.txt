### üìù Detailed Explanations (English)

* **Q1 - Correct Answer: A (Compute):** While an *instance* is a specific resource, **Compute** is the general term for the processing power (CPU and RAM) provided by remote physical servers.
* **Q2 - Correct Answer: A (Technology that creates a virtual version...):** By definition, virtualization is the process of creating virtual representations of hardware. Option B describes a *Hypervisor*, which is the tool used to achieve virtualization, but not the concept itself.
* **Q3 - Correct Answers: C and D:** Containers are highly **portable** (C) and **efficient** (D) because they do not include a guest OS, unlike VMs. Note: Containers are *immutable* (they shouldn't be modified, making A wrong) and they *separate* responsibilities (making E wrong).
* **Q4 - Correct Answer: C (Type 1):** Type 1 (Bare Metal) hypervisors run directly on the hardware. This reduces the attack surface because there is no "host OS" that could be compromised, making it the most secure choice for a company prioritizing security.
